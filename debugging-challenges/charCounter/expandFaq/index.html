How many moons does Jupiter have?

Jupiter has 95 confirmed moons as of 2025.

The four largest are called the Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto.

Which is the largest moon of Jupiter?

Ganymede is the largest moon of Jupiter.

It is even bigger than Mercury, with a diameter of about 5,268 km.

Are Jupiter’s moons visible from Earth?

Yes, the Galilean moons can be seen with a small telescope or even good binoculars.

They appear as tiny points of light orbiting Jupiter.

Why are Jupiter’s moons important?

They help scientists study planetary formation and the possibility of life beyond Earth.

Europa, for example, may have a subsurface ocean that could harbor life.
